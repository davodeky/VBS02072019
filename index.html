Sunday's Sermon
  • The Israelites did not have an experience of war before that.
  • God did not tell Moses to lift up his hand- but he noticed that every time he lifted his hand they won the battle.
  • We can all imagine how Moses looked at his people, how dwarfed or ill-prepared they could have been in comparison to the Amalekites.
  • Moses could have lifted his hand and prayed- telling God that my people don't know how to fight.
  • It is then that Moses realized that they could only win the battle if he kept his hand up.
  • We are being encouraged not to look at the situation we may be in, but lift our hands high- for our help comes from God.
  • God qualifies the unqualified, He heals the sick.
  • If we know where our help comes from, we would not look around wondering who will help us.
  • We should not give the devil a heads-up; we should not give him time to win- there is victory in Jesus.
  • AS long as Moses' hand was up, they had victory.
  • God was taking an ordinary person in the battle field and mking him a warrior.
  • When God takes over, victory is not negotiable!/li>
  • If one man could help win the battle, how much more coulf three or four or five achieve?
  • God is a God who saves an individual, but He sets people in a team for victory.
  • God created individuals, but He put them together for dominion.
  • When Aaron and Hur saw what was happening, they realized they could be participants in the glorious moment they knew the voctory wasn't Moses', but that of their nation.
  • Individualism, egoism and all the "isms" can kill one if they fail to see the power of togetherness.
  • In our individual lives, we are our own Moses and therefore we should keep our hands up.
  • In our families, there is a Moses; if you don't know who that may be, identify him and hold his hand high. For if Moses wins, we all win.
  • The Lord needed Moses to raise his hands up, but He needed other people to help Moses do that.
  • Alone you may win a fight, but together we win battles.

Disciplines For The Disciple

Introduction to Spiritual Discipline

Introduction

  1. In 1 Timothy 4:7, Paul exhorts Timothy to "exercise yourself toward godliness."
  2. What does this mean?
  3. What is the value of such spiritual exercise as seen in 1 Timothy 4:8?
  4. The discipline for the disciple, therefore, is to engage in various "exercises" or "disciplines" that cultivate godliness or piety.

The Concept of Spiritual Discipline

  1. Definition:
    Spiritual discipline are spiritual exercises that one engages in habitually which bring one closer to God and thus become more godly in character and behavior.
  2. Examples of Spiritual Disciplines:
    1. Prayer - in which one spends time talking to God
    2. Meditation- in which one contamplates on God's word, or other things worthy of such attention
    3. Fasting- usually accompanied with prayer, in which one abstaines from food.
    4. Singing- by which one can praise God and be edified.
    5. Giving- by which we can please God and be blessed.
    6. Assembling- where we can exhort one another.
    7. Hospitality- showing kindness to strangers, which often results in a blessing.
    8. Teaching- which usually benefits the teacher more than the student.
The Concept of Discipleship
  1. What does the word "disciple" mean?
    -Disciples were spoken of as imitators of their teachers.
  2. What is the goal of being a disciple?
    - To be more godly
